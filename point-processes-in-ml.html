Point Processes in ML

Date Tue 08 April 2014 Tags ml

The following is an excerpt from a presentation by David Harte (SRA, Wellington) titled "Modelling NZ Seismicity with the ETAS Model" (in 2011):

  • The "Epidemic Type Aftershock Sequence" (ETAS) Model is a point process model used in seismology. It models mainshock-aftershock sequences like an epidemic where an infected individual passes the disease on to others (direct offspring or 1st generation), who in turn pass it on further (2nd and future generations). Given certain conditions, the aftershock sequence, the epidemic, will eventually die out.

  • Point Processes:

  • History of process

    $$H_t = \{(t_i,w_i,x_i,y_i) \; \forall i: t_i < t\}$$
    (the tuple represents time, magnitude, longitude and latitude of event i)

  • Conditional intensity
    $$\lambda(t,w,x,y\|H_t) = \lim_{\tau,\xi,\psi,\eta \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\tau\xi\psi\eta}P(N_{\tau\xi\psi\eta}(t,w,x,y) > 0 \| H_t)$$

  • Here,

    $$N_{\tau\xi\psi\eta}(t,w,x,y)$$
    is the number of events in
    $$[t,t+\tau)\times[w,w+\xi)\times[x,x+\psi)\times[y,y+\eta)$$

  • Marked Point Process:

  • $$\lambda(t,w,x,y\|H_t) = \lambda_g(t\|H_t)f(w,x,y)$$
    where the first term is called the ground intensity and the second term is called the mark density.

  • Log likelihood

    $$\log L \propto \sum_{i \in I}\log\lambda(t_i,w_i,x_i,y_i\|H_{t_i})$$

  • Form of ETAS models:

  • $$\lambda(t,w,x,y\|H_t) = \nu(t,w,x,y \| H_t) + \sum_{i:t_i < t}\Big[Ae^{\alpha w_i - d}(1+\frac{t - t_i}{c})^{-p}\Big]g_i(w,x,y) $$
  • The first additive term accounts for background intensity
  • The second term is a sum of functions of past events and models aftershocks.
