Canoncial Correlation Analysis (CCA)

Date Wed 15 October 2014 Tags ml

What is is?

A technique to investigate the relationship between two sets of variables.

Why should I know about it?

Not fully sure. There is a NIPS'14 paper by Lu et al. called "large scale canonical correlation analysis with iterative least squares" for one. I have also hear about it more than once during my random walk through StatsLand, so I am shamelessly rephrasing what I read up on Wikipedia a bit earlier. I feel that it is an interesting tool to have in one's toolset.

Reference?

Wikipedia!

Tell me the trick!

Let

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

be two random vectors (in

$$n$$

and

$$m$$

dimensions respectively). Our objective is to make sense of their cross-correlation. CCA is a procedure to find linear combinations of the coordinates of

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

that correlate maximally with each other. Lets call these linear functions of

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

canonical variables.

Once we do that, we can also try to find another pair of linear combinations of coordinates of

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

, which are maximally correlated with each other but uncorrelated with the first pair of canonical variables. And this can be repeated as necessary.

Lets get back to finding the first pair of canonical variables. In particular, we are searching for linear combination coefficients

$$a$$

and

$$b$$

such that canonical variable

$$a^TX$$

is maximally correlated with canonical variable

$$b^TY$$

. There are two scenarios:

  • If you knew the distributions of

    $$X$$
    and
    $$Y$$
    , you could access analytically/computationally, the covariance and cross-covariance matrices (
    $$\Sigma_{XX},\Sigma_{XY},\Sigma_{YY}$$
    ) that will be needed as inputs in our CCA procedure.

  • On the other hand, if you were given their examples instead, you could compute empirical covariance and cross-covariance matrices (again

    $$\Sigma_{XX},\Sigma_{XY},\Sigma_{YY}$$
    ) and try to work with them instead.

In either case, we would like to get to an optimization problem formulation and solve for the linear combination coefficients (

$$a$$

and

$$b$$

) (if not a math program, any other algorithmic approach would do as well). Here is the math program attempt: We find

$$a,b$$

such that

$$\frac{a^T \Sigma_{XY} b}{\sqrt{a^T \Sigma_{XX}a }\sqrt{b^T\Sigma_{YY}b}}$$

is maximized.

There are some closed form expressions for what

$$a,b$$

are on the reference above. The way they arrive at these expressions is via the use of Cauchy-Schwarz inequality (

$$c^Td \leq \|c\|_2\|d\|_2 $$

) and noticing that maximizing the correlation after applying Cauchy-Schwarz inequality looks like the Rayleigh quotient expression for maximal eigenvalue (which is equal to

$$\max_{z} \frac{(z^TAz)^{1/2}}{\|z\|_2}$$

).

So I found

$$a,b$$

. What can I do now?

Well, by computing

$$a,b$$

, we have found a proxy/measure of what is common between vectors

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

. Such knowledge can be used to understand the relationship between the coordinates of

$$X$$

and

$$Y$$

.
